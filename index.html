The War in Ukraine

The Ukraine-Russia conflict has a history of over 30 years, with its roots dating back to the 19th Century. The ongoing hostility, which began in February, has at its core Putinesque revanchism colliding with Ukrainian ideals of self-determination. This interactive feature offers an overview of the war, its actors and the suffering it has wrought.

Timeline of events

Invasion underway

Russia launched a full-scale attack on Ukraine on February 24. So far, Moscow has been denied the victory it anticipated, and has failed to capture major cities across Ukraine, including capital city Kyiv. It has been weighed down by an ill-prepared military and has faced tenacious resistance from Ukrainian soldiers and civilian resistance fighters. Ukraine declared martial law, as citizens took refuge in underground bunkers. It has cut diplomatic ties with Russia, and offered weapons to anyone who wants them.

Refugees

Ukraine became the third-largest source of refugees in the world soon after the invasion. As of April 22, over 3.5 million people were displaced. Occupying the top two spots on the list of refugee sources are the Syrian Arab Republic (6.8 million) and Venezuela (4.1 million). The data refers to 2021 figures except for Ukraine.

Civilian casualties

According to UNHRC data, over 2,000 lives have been lost in the ongoing war in Ukraine. A total of 2,435 individuals died, while 2946 have been injured. Nearly 70 children are also said to have lost their lives.

Arsenal

Putin's army is much mightier in terms of numbers on almost all fronts. The size of the Russian army units involved in the Ukraine war is far bigger than that of the Ukrainian army as per IISS data. Russian forces have almost double the number of army personnel Ukraine does. Tanks and artillery are three-fold that of Ukraine. The overall ammunition too is multifold than Ukraine, as per the data.

Source : International Institute for Strategic Studies

Sanctions

Countries and blocs from all across the globe have sanctioned Russia after the invasion of Ukraine. Among these are the EU, USA, Japan and UK, and the nature of these punitive measures include trade restrictions, removal of Russia from the international payments system (SWIFT) and freezing of Russian assets in these countries.

History

The Ukraine-Russia conflict has a history of over 30 years, even while its roots date back to the 19th Century. The Ukrainian natives have fought off Soviets and Germans for centuries. However, the conflict with Russia sparked when the issue of NATO membership came to the fore. Neighbours of Ukraine — Poland and Hungary — were inducted into NATO in 1999, and membership for Ukraine was also on the cards. Russia strongly opposed Ukraine’s acceptance of EU membership back in 2013. After plenty of sabre-rattling and brinkmanship, Russia finally invaded Ukraine in February this year.
